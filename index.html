METHODOLOGY How the previous-results numbers are computed

Most "track records" you see in this industry are useless because of three quiet flaws — look-ahead bias, retrofitted entry criteria, and signal repainting. Dragonfly's previous-results numbers don't have any of them.

  • Zero look-ahead. Every entry uses only the data that was publicly available on the date of the catalyst. Deciding whether VRT was a buy signal in April 2020 uses what was knowable on April 1, 2020 — not what happened later.
  • Real triggers only. Every entry is anchored to a specific, dated public event — a DOE grant, an SEC Form 4 filing, an NRC licensing announcement, a Substantial Holder filing. The same trigger types the live system uses to fire alerts today. No discretionary "this looked great in retrospect" picks.
  • Zero repainting. Entry dates, stop levels, and conviction tier are locked to the catalyst date and never adjusted afterward to look better. Losers stay losers. ENPH is in the table at −58% precisely because we don't quietly delete the ones that didn't work.
  • Same exit framework as live. The "system strategy" returns come from the same auto-selected exit ladder (staged exits / trailing stop / buy & hold) that live signals will use — not a post-hoc optimum chosen per ticker.

That doesn't make backtest numbers equivalent to live performance — nothing does. But it does mean the methodology you're reading about is the same one we'll use going forward, not a different system retrofitted to flatter old data.

How we handle the losses

47% of tracked theses have underperformed the S&P 500 over the same period. That's shown clearly in the full track record — not buried, not explained away. Here's what keeps losses contained when a thesis breaks:

Pre-defined exit conditions
Every thesis has a specific invalidation trigger set before entry — a regulatory reversal, a price target breach, a supply-demand shift. When the trigger hits, the signal fires to exit. No hoping it comes back.
Position sizing discipline
No single thesis gets an oversized allocation. Signals rate conviction on a 1–5 scale. A 3-star signal gets a smaller position than a 5-star — even if the upside looks the same on paper.
Thesis integrity over ego
If the data that built the thesis changes — not the price, the actual underlying data — the thesis gets flagged for review immediately. A position that's down but still thesis-intact stays. One that's up but thesis-broken gets flagged to exit.
Sector diversification
30+ distinct themes across 9+ macro sectors — uranium, gold, copper, lithium, AI infrastructure, space, quantum, biodefense, water, and more. A bad year in lithium doesn't wipe out what uranium does. Losses are isolated by design, not by luck.
Crypto as a macro cycle lens
BTC's ~4-year halving cycle maps closely to broader risk-on / risk-off rotations across equities, commodities, and capital flows. We track BTC, ETH, and crypto market structure alongside traditional macro data — not as trading signals, but as an early indicator of where institutional money is moving and when risk appetite is expanding or contracting.

About

Built by someone who actually trades it.

Hello. I’ve spent most of my life doing carpentry — picked up tools the moment I was old enough to work. I never wanted it to be my career, or at least never wanted to need it to be. There's a big difference between doing something because you want to and doing it because you have to. What I always really wanted was to get to a place where I had that choice.

It took over 15 years to actually figure it out. I'm not a hedge fund manager. I'm not a financial advisor. This is educational — I just track the sectors I believe in, let the data do the talking, and hope the track record speaks for itself. I'm not here to sell anybody on anything. I'm not a fan of pushy salespeople either.

I built Dragonfly Systems over the years because I got tired of tracking all of this manually. The system does it now. I'm sharing it because I think more people should have access to this kind of research — and because keeping a public track record keeps me honest.

More than anything, I wanted to build a community that can learn and thrive together — so more of us have the financial freedom to do what we love, with less stress. After many years of building this quietly, this is the beginning of that — and I’m genuinely excited to finally open it up to the world.

The name comes from the dragonfly — reportedly the most effective hunter in the natural world, with a strike rate around 95%. For comparison: lions close roughly 25% of hunts, great white sharks around 50%, cougars 20–30%. It's not brute force. It's precision. That's what I wanted the system to reflect — not more signals, better ones.

By all means, feel free to give me feedback — I'm all ears. Cheers.
